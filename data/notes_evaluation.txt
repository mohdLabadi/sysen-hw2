Cross Rates, Data Feeds, and APIs

A cross rate expresses one currency in terms of another without using the U.S. dollar as an intermediate, though in practice many desks still triangulate through USD for consistency. If you know USD/JPY and EUR/USD, you can derive EUR/JPY; automated systems must handle rounding and timestamp alignment carefully.

Market participants consume rates from data vendors, central banks, or open HTTP APIs. For teaching demos, ECB-oriented public feeds (for example Frankfurter) expose daily reference rates suitable for examples—always document the rate date and that retail spreads differ from mid quotes.

Hedging and translation: companies with overseas revenues use forwards or options to manage FX risk; accountants distinguish transaction exposure from translation of foreign subsidiaries’ statements. For this course’s agent exercise, focus on clear definitions, live numeric examples from the API tool, and never mix a rate from one source with a label from another without saying so.
